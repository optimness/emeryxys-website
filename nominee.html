What is a Nominee Director?

In Singapore, every company must have at least one locally resident director under the rules of the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA).

A nominee director is a person appointed to satisfy this requirement, but they do not manage the business day-to-day unless separately engaged to do so.

How a Nominee Director Supports Your Singapore Business

A nominee director in Singapore is a locally resident individual appointed to help a company meet legal requirements. Every Singapore company must have at least one resident director under the rules of the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA). This role is especially important for foreign entrepreneurs who do not live in Singapore. The nominee director allows the company to be incorporated even if the owner is based overseas. Their main purpose is to fulfil compliance rather than manage the business. They may sign statutory documents and ensure legal filings are completed correctly. However, they usually do not take part in day-to-day operations or decision-making. The arrangement is often temporary until the business owner obtains residency or a valid work pass. A nominee director helps maintain the company’s good standing with regulators and banks. Overall, they provide a legal bridge that enables foreign-owned businesses to operate in Singapore.
